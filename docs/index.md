# SOC Fundamentals

A personal learning journal documenting my path toward becoming a SOC analyst.

---

## Why I started this

There is something about watching a screen full of live alerts and knowing that behind each one is a real person trying to get into something they should not — and that your job is to catch them. That is what pulled me toward the blue team. Not just the theory of it, but the idea that you can actually sit there, watch an attack happen in real time, and stop it.

I am a cybersecurity student focused on the defensive side. I have built secure web applications, set up SIEM environments, and configured firewalls — but this repo is different. This is me slowing down and making sure I actually understand the fundamentals that sit underneath all of that.

---

## What this repo is

A structured 12-week curriculum I am working through week by week. Each folder has notes from what I studied, a lab writeup of what I actually did, and  reflections on what clicked and what did not.

---

## The roadmap

Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–3)
- Week 01: SOC Roles, Workflows and the CIA Triad
- Week 02: Networking Fundamentals for Defenders
- Week 03: Log Analysis and Windows/Linux Basics

Phase 2 — Core Tools (Weeks 4–6)
- Week 04: Introduction to SIEM (Splunk / Wazuh)
- Week 05: Intrusion Detection Systems (Snort / Suricata)
- Week 06: Packet Analysis with Wireshark

Phase 3 — Threat Knowledge (Weeks 7–9)
- Week 07: MITRE ATT&CK Framework
- Week 08: Common Attack Types (Phishing, Ransomware, Lateral Movement)
- Week 09: Indicators of Compromise vs TTPs

Phase 4 — Hands-On Practice (Weeks 10–12)
- Week 10: TryHackMe SOC Level 1 Labs
- Week 11: LetsDefend Alert Triage Scenarios
- Week 12: Capstone — Full Incident Response Walkthrough

## Before you start

You do not need to be an expert, but these basics will make everything easier to follow:

| Topic | Why it matters | Free resource |
|---|---|---|
| Basic Networking (IP, DNS, HTTP) | Almost every alert involves network traffic | [Professor Messer's CompTIA Network+](https://www.professormesser.com/) |
| Command Line (Windows + Linux) | Log analysis and tools are CLI-heavy | [OverTheWire: Bandit](https://overthewire.org/wargames/bandit/) |
| How an OS works (processes, files, users) | Understanding attacker behavior requires OS knowledge | [TryHackMe: Pre-Security Path](https://tryhackme.com/path/outline/presecurity) |
